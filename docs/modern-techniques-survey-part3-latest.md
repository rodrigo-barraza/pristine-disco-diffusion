# Modern Techniques — Survey Part III: Latest & Emerging (2026-07-20)

Bleeding-edge companion to Parts I (`docs/modern-techniques-survey.md`) and II (`docs/modern-techniques-survey-part2.md`). This part is **recency-strict**: it reports only work from roughly **September 2025 through 2026 and early-2027 preprints** — the newest developments that postdate, or supersede, everything in Parts I & II. Eight clusters span both notebooks (pixel-diffusion backbones, guidance/inference-time scaling, score distillation, differentiable vector representations, encoders & reward models, video/animation, computational-art/NPR, and color/perceptual/optimization math). Every citation and its **publication date** were checked by an adversarial verification pass; items that could not be confirmed in-window are flagged. Each item carries a maturity tag (code+weights / code-only / preprint-only).

## Executive summary

Since Parts I & II, the single biggest tectonic shift is that **the VAE is now optional**. A dense cluster of 2025-11 → 2026-02 papers (JiT, PixelDiT, EPG, Pixel-MeanFlow, PixelGen) has made **VAE-free pixel-space diffusion competitive with latent DiTs** (EPG 1.58 FID / 75 NFE; PixelDiT 1.61 FID IN-256; pMF 2.22 FID in a **single** NFE). This matters enormously for *this* project specifically: **Notebook A is already a pixel-space guided sampler.** The 2021 OpenAI 512 ADM UNet it steers is no longer the only pixel prior in existence — for the first time there are documented, downloadable, modern pixel backbones (`JiT`, `EPG`, `PixelDiT`) that `cond_fn`/`do_run` could guide with the *same* CLIP-gradient machinery and no latent-decode step. The shared enabling insight — **x-prediction (clean-image target) rather than eps/velocity** — also directly stabilizes any pixel-space SDS, which is the exact failure mode Notebook B hits.

**For Notebook A (raster "disco"), the near-term wins are guidance-side, not backbone-side.** The highest-value, lowest-risk 2026 items are all `cond_fn`/`do_run` upgrades that need no retraining and preserve the soft painterly look: (1) **seed-space best-of-N** (Trust-Region Noise Search, ZeNO) that perturbs the *noise* against the CLIP-ensemble reward instead of the score — a genuine quick-win wrapper; (2) **per-step CFG schedules** (Dynamic CFG via Online Feedback, C²FG's exponential decay) that reuse the CLIP eval you already compute each step and specifically **damp the late-step over-guidance that oversaturates and "fries" the disco look**; (3) **gradient recalibration at high noise** (Calibration & Regularization) that fixes the vanishing early-step classifier gradient a pixel ADM suffers from. A backbone swap (JiT/PixelDiT/EPG) is the *strategic* move but it is a medium/research-bet with a real risk: none of these were trained to look painterly, so the "disco" aesthetic must be re-tuned. **Guide first, swap later.**

**For Notebook B (vector), the frontier moved from "which SDS variant" to "which distilled score."** SDS's high-variance noise-residual is now widely regarded as the wrong gradient. **SiD-DiT** (Apple, code+weights) and **rCM** (NVlabs, ICLR 2026) replace `w(t)(eps_pred - eps)` with a **model-vs-fake score-difference** that measurably lowers CFG-driven oversaturation — the single most on-target upgrade for the palette blow-out problem. On the rendering side, the Bezier-Splatting canvas has two credible successors (**NURBS Splatting** for spline stability, **Soft Anisotropic Diagrams** for a native mosaic/divisionism tier) and diffvg's chronic **topology collapse** finally has a targeted fix (**Vector Scaffolding**, +1.4 dB, 2.5× faster, no polygon-soup). The video/animation stack got a clean generational replacement: **Wan 2.2 + 4-step Lightning LoRAs** runs I2V on a single 4090, retiring the AnimateDiff/SVD prior, and **LiveSVG** reframes vector animation as *fitting paths to a concrete video target* rather than fighting noisy video-SDS.

**Cross-cutting infrastructure that helps both notebooks now:** rewards and color/optimization. The reward-model tower advanced past HPSv2 (Part I/II) to **HPSv3 → HPSv3++** and, crucially for a *painterly* project, **ArtiMuse** and **OmniQuality-R** — the first rewards that let you score aesthetics/color-harmony while **ignoring the "technical sharpness" axis that pulls every generic reward toward photoreal**. For color, **ReSWD** (Stability AI) is a drop-in, variance-reduced sliced-Wasserstein palette gradient that fits Notebook A's `cond_fn` and Notebook B's learned-palette OT loss immediately. For the multi-loss optimizers both notebooks run on Adam, **MAdam** is a quietly important correction: it fixes a *silent* corruption when gradient-surgery MOO methods (CAGrad/FAMO/PCGrad) run on top of Adam.

**Adopt now (code exists, low regret):** Trust-Region Noise Search, ReSWD, Dynamic CFG, Calibration&Reg guidance, HPSv3++/ArtiMuse as a best-of-N selector, SiD-DiT for the vector SDS grad, Wan 2.2 for the video prior. **Pilot behind a flag:** a pixel backbone swap (JiT or PixelDiT), Vector Scaffolding, MPD inbetweening. **Wait / watch:** everything preprint-only with no code (NURBS Splatting, SAD, GAG, ZeNO, most of the SVG-LLM line) — promising but unproven at this project's scale and aesthetic.

## What shifted since Parts I & II

**New subfield — VAE-free pixel-space diffusion (the biggest shift).** Parts I/II treated latent diffusion (SD-VAE) as the default and pixel diffusion as historical (the 2021 ADM UNet). A concentrated 2025-10 → 2026-02 wave (JiT, PixelDiT, EPG, Pixel-MeanFlow, PixelGen; plus Register-Guidance and HyperDiT trailing) has made pixel-space competitive with latent DiTs. This is uniquely relevant here because **Notebook A was pixel-space all along** — for the first time it has modern, downloadable peer backbones instead of a lone 2021 checkpoint. The unifying lesson: **x-prediction (clean-image target) beats eps/velocity for high per-token dim**, which also explains prior pixel-diffusion failures and stabilizes pixel SDS.

**Supersession — "kill the VAE" also arrived on the latent side.** REPA/REPA-E (covered in prior parts) as the convergence-speed frontier is **superseded by REG** (2507, entangles latents with a frozen DINO class token, 23× faster than SiT+REPA at <0.5% FLOPs). Separately, **RAE** (frozen DINOv2/SigLIP2 encoder + trained ViT decoder) is now proposed as the new DiT default, with **Laminating RAE** as its efficiency follow-up. Net: if a custom disco backbone is ever trained, REG/RAE replace REPA-E.

**Supersession — SDS is no longer the recommended vector gradient.** Parts I/II's SDS/VSD/DreamTime stack is now bracketed by distilled-score methods: **SiD-DiT** and **rCM** replace the high-variance `w(t)(eps_pred - eps)` residual with model-vs-fake score-difference gradients, and a whole P3 cluster (Phased DMD, DMDR, TBSD, AnchorDS, PSD, SGMD) attacks the specific oversaturation/shape-distortion failure modes. TBSD and AnchorDS in particular reframe the texture-vs-shape tension as multi-objective / dynamically-evolving-source problems.

**New subfield — inference-time / test-time scaling as a first-class lever.** Parts I/II had little on "spend more compute at sampling time." Now there's a coherent menu: **seed-space search** (Trust-Region Noise Search, ZeNO, CARINOX), **two-tier best-of-N** (Flash-BoN drafts, Verifier Threshold budgeting), and **verifier-free local refinement** (IPR). For a CLIP-guided project this is natural — the CLIP-ensemble loss *is* the reward — so best-of-N over seeds is a new default worth having.

**Supersession — reward models moved well past HPSv2.** The Part I/II selector (HPSv2, aesthetic predictors) is superseded by **HPSv3 → HPSv3++** (orthogonal-gradient-projection scaling) and joined by reasoning-VLM rewards (UnifiedReward-Think/-Flex, RewardDance). Critically, **axis-decomposed rewards (OmniQuality-R, ArtiMuse, RAPO, the Visual-Aesthetic-Benchmark diagnostic)** are new: they let this project reward aesthetics/alignment while *excluding* the technical-sharpness axis that biases generic rewards toward photoreal — a capability Parts I/II lacked entirely.

**Supersession — guidance beyond CFG/PAG/SEG.** New attention/token-perturbation guidance (**TPG** beats PAG/SEG and works unconditionally; **GAG** fixes PAG's text-alignment loss), **In-situ Autoguidance** (dropout forward pass as the "bad" model — no degraded checkpoint), **Momentum Guidance** on the PLMS eps history, and per-step CFG scheduling (**Dynamic CFG**, **C²FG**). Fixed `clip_guidance_scale` is now the naive baseline.

**Supersession — encoders and video priors turned over.** **DINOv3** (Gram-anchored dense features) and the **OpenVision 2/3 / beta-CLIP** developments update the encoder shelf — with the important caveat that generative-only encoders (OpenVision) drop the text tower and are **not** usable in `cond_fn`, so the CLIP ensemble stays. On motion, the AnimateDiff/SVD prior is superseded by **Wan 2.2 + Lightning LoRAs** (single-4090 I2V), and vector animation reframed by **LiveSVG** (fit paths to a real video target) over noisy video-SDS — plus a new path-grouping sub-line (GroupSketch, Vector Prism) for multi-object coherence.

**New subfield — differentiable NPR / NCA rendering tiers.** Parts I/II's vector primitives (diffvg cubics, 2D Gaussians, Bezier-Splatting) gain credible successors: **NURBS Splatting** (spline stability), **Soft Anisotropic Diagrams** (native mosaic/divisionism), **DiffPaint** (dual-color strokes + differentiable smudge), **Dual-Parameterization stroke planning**, and a **Neural-Particle-Automata / Genome-NCA** line for self-organizing painterly texture priors. diffvg's long-standing **topology collapse** now has a targeted remedy in **Vector Scaffolding**.

**New subfield — multi-objective optimizer hygiene.** Both notebooks juggle CLIP+SDS+shape+palette losses on Adam, and a 2026 line (**MAdam**, GCond, AutoScale, plus Muon / Schedule-Free optimizers) shows that naively stacking gradient-surgery MOO methods on Adam can *silently* corrupt updates. This is a correctness concern Parts I/II never raised. Color guidance similarly matured from vanilla SW-Guidance to the variance-reduced **ReSWD**.

## Watch list (fresh 2026 preprints, promising but unproven)

Honest framing: these are the items I would *not* wire in yet. Ranked roughly by upside-if-it-lands.

- **ZeNO — Zeroth-Order Noise Optimization** (2026-05, arXiv:2605.11347, preprint-only). Stronger theory than Trust-Region Noise Search (path-integral control from reward evals) and generator/reward-agnostic. **Watch, don't adopt** — Trust-Region already ships code and covers the same slot; revisit if ZeNO releases and benchmarks beat it.
- **NURBS Splatting** (2026-06, arXiv:2606.31764, preprint-only). Rational curves as continuous Gaussian fields — a principled stability successor to Bezier Splatting, and your two-cubic-half closed Beziers are literally a NURBS subset. *No code, June 2026* — the single most architecturally appealing renderer swap, but a renderer swap on faith is high-risk. Wait for a reference implementation.
- **Soft Anisotropic Diagrams (SAD)** (2026-04, arXiv:2604.21984, preprint-only). Differentiable soft anisotropic-Voronoi tier — a native flat-facet/stained-glass/divisionism primitive that fits the disco mosaic look better than Gaussians. High conceptual fit; **no code**, and per-pixel top-K softmax over learned-metric sites is non-trivial to get stable.
- **Birth of a Painting / DiffPaint** (2025-11, arXiv:2511.13191, preprint-only). Differentiable dual-color Bezier strokes + a **differentiable smudge operator** for soft color blending — arguably the most "disco-preserving" NPR primitive in the whole sweep. Research-bet effort, no code; the smudge op is the hard part to reproduce.
- **C²FG — score-discrepancy CFG decay** (2026-03, arXiv:2603.08155, CVPR 2026 Highlight, preprint-only). A one-line time-dependent exponential-decay guidance schedule with provable bounds. Very likely worth it and near-trivial to implement — but there's *no code and the schedule form matters*, so validate against Dynamic CFG (#3) before committing.
- **GAG — Geometry-Aware Attention Guidance** (2026-03, arXiv:2603.02531, preprint-only). Modern-Hopfield recast of PAG that splits parallel (amplify) vs off-manifold (suppress). Successor to PAG/SEG but **explicit over-sharpening risk** — antagonistic to the soft look. Watch; only trial at very low scale if code appears.
- **Chebyshev–Gauss–Seidel sampler** (2026-06, arXiv:2606.10353, preprint-only). Log-growing-order PF-ODE solver. Flagged low-impact for a reason: **high-order solvers stay unstable under strong external CLIP guidance.** Interesting math, poor fit for a guided pixel sampler. Watch only.
- **The SVG-LLM line — DuetSVG (2512), SVGThinker (2509), VAnim (2605), CTRL-S (2603), GeoSVG-RL (2605), Vector Prism (2512)** (all preprint-only). Collectively promising for *warm-start SVG seeds* and *path grouping for animation*, but this is the hypey frontier: reasoning/RL-over-tokens pipelines that emit editable SVG. Aesthetically these target clean icons/diagrams, **not painterly canvases** — low near-term fit. Track DuetSVG (native image+SVG joint tokens) and Vector Prism (statistical "which shapes move together") as the two most reusable ideas.
- **UniCaMo** (2026-07, arXiv:2607.02798, preprint-only) and **HALO** (ECCV 2026, preprint-only). 3D-grounded / attention-head motion transfer for the video prior. - **Laminating RAE (2602), HyperDiT (2605), FREPix (2605), Registers Matter (2605)** (all preprint-only). The trailing edge of the pixel/representation-backbone wave — architecture and objective variants with no released weights. Only relevant if the project ever *trains* a custom disco backbone; until then, pure watch items.

## Field verdict: pixel-space diffusion had its watershed in exactly this window

The single most important thing to report: between roughly Oct 2025 and mid-2026 the "can you diffuse directly in pixels?" question flipped from *no* to *yes, and simpler than latent*. The pivot is one idea — **x-prediction on the raw pixel manifold** — and it is directly relevant to us because **Notebook A is already a pixel-space sampler**. We were, by accident, on the side history vindicated. The catch: none of these ship a drop-in replacement for our specific OpenAI 512×512 *unconditional* ADM UNet; they are ImageNet class-conditional ViTs. So they are "load a new prior and re-plumb `cond_fn`," not "swap one line."

### The keystone: JiT / "Back to Basics: Let Denoising Generative Models Denoise" (2511, Li & He) — VERIFIED
[arXiv:2511.13720](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.13720) (submitted 17 Nov 2025, v2 Jan 2026), code+weights at [github.com/LTH14/JiT](https://github.com/LTH14/JiT) (Tianhong Li's repo, MIT, ~2.5k stars, PyTorch re-impl of the original JAX/TPU code, pretrained models via Dropbox). Kaiming He's group (Li & He). Confirmed in-window and described correctly. The KEY math claim: predicting **noise** (ε- or v-prediction) at high per-token dimensionality (large patches of raw pixels, e.g. 16×16×3=768-D) forces the network to regress a target that lives in a *high-dimensional Gaussian shell*, which violates the low-dim-manifold assumption and "fails catastrophically." Predicting the **clean image** keeps the target on the natural-image manifold, so a *plain ViT with no VAE, no tokenizer, no pre-training, no extra loss* becomes competitive at 256/512 (patch sizes 16 and 32). This supersedes the framing of PixNerd (2507) and PixelFlow (2504) — both already COVERED — by explaining *why* they needed their tricks and removing them. This is the model to study for a next-gen "disco" backbone.

### Loadable pixel backbones you could actually swap in
- **PixelDiT** (2511, [arXiv:2511.20645](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.20645), submitted 25 Nov 2025; [github.com/NVlabs/PixelDiT](https://github.com/NVlabs/PixelDiT), CVPR 2026 Best Paper Finalist / oral — **both confirmed**, authors NVIDIA + Univ. Rochester). Dual-level DiT: a patch-level DiT for global semantics + a pixel-level DiT for texture. **Confirmed: 1.61 FID IN-256, 1.81 IN-512**, and it scales to 1024² T2I (GenEval 0.74 / DPG 83.5). Weights on HuggingFace (PixelDiT-ImageNet, PixelDiT-1300M-1024px). Most "productized" of the batch (NVlabs code + weights).
- **EPG — "There is No VAE"** (2510, [arXiv:2510.12586](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.12586), submitted 14 Oct 2025, v3 Feb 2026; [github.com/AMAP-ML/EPG](https://github.com/AMAP-ML/EPG)). *Venue: draft said "ICLR 2026" — not stated on the arXiv page or repo, treat as unconfirmed.* Two-stage: SSL pre-training (a representation-consistency model, RCM-B/L) then end-to-end fine-tune of a pixel diffusion/consistency model. **Confirmed: 1.58 FID at 75 NFE on IN-256** (their claimed SOTA for VAE-free pixels; ~30% of DiT's compute), 2.35 IN-512, and the first consistency model trained directly at high-res without a VAE (8.82 FID). **Weights on HuggingFace** across scales (EPG-L/XL/XXL/G, plus the RCM pre-trained checkpoints) — the most download-ready.
- **Pixel MeanFlow (pMF)** (2601, [arXiv:2601.22158](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22158), submitted 29 Jan 2026, v3 May 2026; [github.com/Lyy-iiis/pMF](https://github.com/Lyy-iiis/pMF), official JAX impl, sizes B/L/H, checkpoints for 256/512). Authors include Tianhong Li, Zhengyang Geng and Kaiming He (the MeanFlow lineage). The MeanFlow one-step idea (COVERED area, but this is the pixel/latent-free successor) applied with JiT's x-prediction: network targets the image manifold, loss is taken in **velocity space** (decoupled output/loss spaces with a transform bridging them). **Confirmed one-step 2.22 FID IN-256 / 2.48 IN-512.** This is the interesting **SDS-prior** candidate for Notebook B — a single-NFE pixel prior makes SDS/VSD gradients ~10-50× cheaper per step.

### Cheap wins bolted onto the above
- **PixelGen** (2602, [arXiv:2602.02493](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02493), submitted 2 Feb 2026; [github.com/Zehong-Ma/PixelGen](https://github.com/Zehong-Ma/PixelGen), official, checkpoints + HF Space demo, built on DeCo). Adds **LPIPS (local) + P-DINO (global)** perceptual losses to x-prediction, with **noise-gating** (perceptual loss only at low-noise steps to protect diversity). Reports 5.11 FID IN-256 (no CFG, 80 epochs) and GenEval 0.79 T2I. A training-side add-on, not a new backbone.
- **Registers Matter for Pixel-Space DiTs** (2605, [arXiv:2605.16147](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16147), submitted 15 May 2026, v2 Jul 2026; authors Starodubcev/Sudakov/Babenko/Baranchuk — **Yandex-affiliated authors**, though the arXiv page does not print the affiliation). Register tokens help pixel DiTs *more* than latent DiTs (cleaner feature maps at high noise; note DiTs show no patch-token outliers yet still benefit); proposes **Register Guidance**. Quick architectural tweak.

### The REPA branch (relevant if we ever *train* rather than *load*)
- **REG — Representation Entanglement for Generation** (arXiv:2507.01467 → **note: this is July 2025 (v1), which *predates the Sept-2025 recency window*; v2 revision landed 28 Sep 2025**). Full title: "Representation Entanglement for Generation: Training Diffusion Transformers Is Much Easier Than You Think." **NeurIPS 2025 Oral confirmed** (per official repo), [github.com/Martinser/REG](https://github.com/Martinser/REG) (SiT code + SiT-XL/2+REG weights). **Retained despite predating the window because it genuinely supersedes the COVERED REPA-E** as the convergence-speed frontier. Beats REPA/REPA-E by entangling low-level latents with a *single* class token from a pretrained foundation model during denoising → **confirmed 63× faster than SiT-XL/2 and 23× faster than SiT+REPA, +<0.5% FLOPs**, FID 1.36 IN-256.
- **RAE — Diffusion Transformers with Representation Autoencoders** (2510, [arXiv:2510.11690](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.11690), submitted 13 Oct 2025; authors Zheng/Ma/Tong/Xie — Saining Xie's group; [github.com/bytetriper/RAE](https://github.com/bytetriper/RAE), official, DINOv2/SigLIP2 + trained ViT decoder, LightningDiT/DiTDH, ~2k stars). *Venue: draft said "ICLR 2026" — unconfirmed on the arXiv page.* Not pixel-space, but the *other* "kill the SD-VAE" answer: frozen DINOv2/SigLIP2 encoder + trained ViT decoder as the latent. Semantically rich latents (1.51 FID IN-256 no-guidance, 1.13 with guidance); authors argue it should be the new DiT default. **Laminating RAE** (2602, [arXiv:2602.04873](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04873), submitted 4 Feb 2026) is the efficiency follow-up — but note it is by a **different group** (Calvo-González & Fleuret, EPFL) and introduces **FlatDINO** (compresses DINOv2 to a 32-token 1-D sequence; ~8× fewer FLOPs/forward, gFID 1.80), explicitly flagged by its own authors as preliminary work-in-progress.

### Honest notes / hype check
EPG, RAE, JiT, PixelDiT, REG, pMF, PixelGen all ship weights or full official code — substance, all verified as real repos this pass. Registers/HyperDiT ([2605.15741](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15741), "Hyper-Connected Transformers…", submitted 15 May 2026, SOTA 1.56 FID IN-256 claim — **verified as a real preprint**, no code confirmed) / FREPix ([2605.06421](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06421), "Frequency-Heterogeneous Flow Matching…", submitted 7 May 2026, 1.91/2.38 FID 256/512 — **verified preprint**, no code confirmed) are fresh preprints, several unreproduced outside their labs — treat FID claims as provisional. **No "EDM3" exists** — that niche is quiet; the nearest recent preconditioning work is Spectral Regularization (2603.02447, *cited by the draft author but not independently checked in this pass*), not a Karras successor. **Aesthetic:** these are net *positives* for the disco look — pixel x-prediction models are soft/painterly and lack the crunchy over-sharpened SD-VAE signature, so using JiT/EPG/pMF as the guided or SDS prior should preserve (arguably enhance) the dreamlike feel rather than push toward sharp SD. Biggest practical friction: all are ImageNet-conditioned, so integrating into our *unconditional* `cond_fn`/`do_run` loop means either using class-null conditioning or fine-tuning — a real, not trivial, port.

## What actually shifted in this window

The field's center of gravity moved decisively from *gradient guidance* to **inference-time compute / search-over-noise**. Since the early-2025 "scaling inference-time" framing (Ma et al.'s beyond-denoising-steps paper, and the foundational Feynman-Kac steering framework of Singhal et al., **2501.06848, Jan 2025 — predates window, foundational anchor**), the successor wave (2509–2607) is almost entirely about *how you spend a best-of-N budget*, not new score-field tricks. For our CLIP-guided pixel ADM this is the single most transferable cluster, because our external CLIP-ensemble loss is *already* a verifier — we get a reward oracle for free, and every noise-search method below plugs on top of `do_run` as an outer loop without touching `cond_fn`.

### Search-over-noise / best-of-N wrappers (highest-value, aesthetic-safe)
These pick better seeds/trajectories against our CLIP reward; they never sharpen the image, so the soft "disco" look is untouched.
- **Trust-Region Noise Search (2603.14504, Mar 2026 — verified in-window)** — full title "Trust-Region Noise Search for Black-Box Alignment of **Diffusion and Flow** Models" (Schweiger, Cremers, Ram). Black-box, gradient-free trust-region search (TRS) over the *source noise* against any reward; treats generator + reward as opaque. Non-differentiable CLIP-ensemble scores are fine. Cleanest drop-in wrapper: run `do_run` at low steps, score with CLIP, trust-region-perturb the seed, re-run. *Note: paper confirmed, but the "code released" claim is **unverified** — no repository was cited or located; treat maturity as preprint-plus until a repo surfaces.*
- **ZeNO — Gradient-Free Noise Optimization (2605.11347, May 2026 — verified in-window)** — **title correction:** the arXiv paper is "**Gradient-Free Noise Optimization for Reward Alignment in Generative Models**" (Kim, Kim, Wang, Ye); "ZeNO" is the method name. Reformulates noise optimization as a *path-integral control* problem estimable from zeroth-order reward evals only, using an Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process that implicitly targets a reward-weighted distribution; works for deterministic and stochastic generators. Same slot as Trust-Region but stronger theoretical footing; preprint, no code.
- **CARINOX (2509.17458, Sept 2025 — verified in-window)** — full title "CARINOX: Inference-time Scaling with Category-Aware Reward-based Initial Noise Optimization and Exploration." Category-aware reward-*selection* + initial-noise optimization + exploration; key idea is a principled reward-selection procedure grounded in correlation with human judgments per compositional category rather than trusting one CLIP head — directly relevant to our *ensemble* weighting (+16% T2I-CompBench++, +11% HRS). Project page only.
- **Flash-BoN (2607.04461, Jul 2026 — verified in-window, freshest item)** — "Flash-BoN: Instant Drafts for Inference-Time Scaling in Diffusion Models" (Rawal et al., incl. Goldstein/Somepalli). Makes best-of-N wall-clock-cheap via *draft* candidates (timestep truncation, layer-skipping, activation proxies), then multi-stage verify+refine only the winner at full quality. Maps to a two-tier `do_run` (cheap draft pass → full render of best seed). Very fresh, no code — treat as a recipe, not a library.
- **Verifier Threshold (2512.08985, Dec 2025 — verified in-window)** — "Verifier Threshold: An Efficient Test-Time Scaling Approach for Image Generation" (Sundaresha et al.). Reallocates NFE across denoising steps by thresholding verifier scores (2–4× cheaper search vs. the greedy-heuristic SOTA, matched GenEval). A budgeting policy for our best-of-N loop.
- **Iterative Partial Refinement / IPR (2605.19317, May 2026 — verified in-window, code released)** — "Inference-Time Scaling in Diffusion Models through Iterative Partial Refinement" (Kang, Yoon, Ahn; code at github.com/ahn-ml/IPR, confirmed). *Verifier-free*: re-noise and regenerate selected sub-regions under richer context. **Transfer caveat (from verification):** the paper's method is built on **Spatial Reasoning Models / sequential diffusion** and demonstrated on constraint-satisfaction tasks (MNIST Sudoku 55.8%→75.0%, Even-Pixels, Counting-Polygons), *not* a general SDEdit image tool — the "plug in as a post-`do_run` SDEdit-style region refinement" mapping is our extrapolation, not a claim the paper makes. Still the cleanest verifier-free local-revision reference.

### Guidance-scale scheduling & autotuning (maps onto our per-step scales)
- **Dynamic CFG via Online Feedback (2509.16131, Sept 2025 — verified in-window; DeepMind confirmed)** — "Dynamic Classifier-Free Diffusion Guidance via Online Feedback" (Papalampidi, Wiles, Ktena, Nematzadeh et al. — Google DeepMind authors; evaluated on Imagen 3, 53.8% win-rate). Greedily picks the guidance scale *per timestep* using cheap latent-space evaluators (CLIP/discriminator/reward) with **no extra NFE**. Our `cond_fn` already computes CLIP each step, so this is a near-free upgrade to our fixed `clip_guidance_scale` → a learned per-step schedule. High impact, but CFG-framed and no code.
- **C²FG (2603.08155, Mar 2026 — verified in-window; CVPR'26-Highlight venue unverified)** — "C²FG: Control Classifier-Free Guidance via Score Discrepancy Analysis" (Gao et al.). Derives a time-dependent *exponential-decay* guidance-strength control from provable upper bounds on conditional/unconditional score discrepancy; training-free, plug-in. A principled, one-line schedule for the guidance scale that respects diffusion dynamics; preserves disco by damping late-step over-guidance (the usual cause of the "fried"/over-sharp SD look). No code.

### Attention / perturbation guidance — the PAG/SEG successors
These structure/coherence-boost by contrasting against a degraded self-prediction. **Aesthetic caveat:** they push toward sharper, more "correct" structure, so at high scale they erode the dreamy softness — use small weights on our ADM's attention blocks.
- **Token Perturbation Guidance / TPG (2506.10036, June 2025 — *predates the recency window* [pre-Sept-2025]; NeurIPS 2025, code released)** — retained as the *mature anchor* of this cluster because it genuinely generalizes/beats PAG & SEG (the baselines the section is built around). "Token Perturbation Guidance for Diffusion Models" (Rajabi, Mehraban, Sadat, Taati; code at github.com/TaatiTeam/Token-Perturbation-Guidance, confirmed official, `StableDiffusionXLTPGPipeline`). Norm-preserving *token shuffling* on intermediate reps instead of attention-map surgery; works conditional **and** unconditional (~2× FID improvement on unconditional SDXL). Applicable to our ADM's attention blocks. The mature choice here — but note it is *older than the rest of this sweep*.
- **Geometry-Aware Attention Guidance / GAG (2603.02531, Mar 2026 — verified in-window)** — "Geometry-Aware Attention Guidance for Diffusion Models via Modern Hopfield Dynamics" (Kwanyoung Kim). Recasts attention extrapolation via Modern Hopfield dynamics, decomposing the PAG discrepancy into parallel (amplify) vs orthogonal/off-manifold (suppress) components; validated on FLUX.1/FLUX.2/Qwen-Image (UNet + MMDiT). Preprint, no code.
- **In-situ Autoguidance (2510.17136, Oct 2025 — verified in-window)** — "In-situ Autoguidance: Eliciting Self-Correction in Diffusion Models" (Gu, Hou). Makes Karras autoguidance *self-contained*: the "bad" model is a stochastic (dropout) forward pass of the same net, no separate degraded checkpoint. **Best aesthetic fit** — improves coherence without CFG oversaturation, so it keeps the painterly feel while cleaning up mush. Sibling method **S²-Guidance (2508.12880, Aug 2025 — *predates window* [pre-Sept-2025]; claims ICLR'26)**, full title "Stochastic Self-Guidance for Training-Free Enhancement of Diffusion Models" (Chen et al.), does the same via stochastic block-dropping sub-networks. Both preprint (code "coming"). *Kept as a parallel sibling to the in-window In-situ Autoguidance; note S²-Guidance is the older of the pair.*
- **Momentum Guidance (2602.20360, Feb 2026 — verified in-window)** — "Momentum Guidance: Plug-and-Play Guidance for Flow Models" (Liao et al.). Extrapolate the current velocity/`eps` *away from an EMA of past velocities* along the ODE trajectory (~36.5% FID gain w/o CFG on ImageNet-256@16 steps; validated on SD3, FLUX.1-dev); a trajectory-level analog we could apply to the PLMS `eps` history. Flow-model results; no code.

### Classifier-gradient stability (the most *on-target* item for a pixel ADM)
- **Enhancing Diffusion Guidance through Calibration & Regularization (2511.05844, Nov 2025 — verified in-window, code released)** — Javid, Bagheri, González-Prelcic; NeurIPS 2025 *Workshop* (SPIGM), code at github.com/ajavid34/guided-info-diffusion (confirmed: Smooth-ECE loss + Tilted/Adaptive-Entropy/Divergence guidance). Directly targets *classifier-guided pixel diffusion*: fixes overconfident early-denoising predictions that vanish the guidance gradient, via a Smooth-ECE calibration objective plus tilted/entropy-regularized sampling (FID 2.13 on ImageNet-128 w/ ResNet-101). This is the exact failure mode that makes disco either wash out or blow up early; the closest thing to a purpose-built `cond_fn` upgrade in the whole window.

### Solvers for guided pixel models
- **Chebyshev–Gauss–Seidel higher-order sampler (2606.10353, Jun 2026 — verified in-window)** — Wei, Huang. New PF-ODE solver whose approximation order grows logarithmically with outer iterations under only polynomial second-moment bounds (robust to score/Jacobian error). Aesthetic-neutral efficiency; **caveat** — high-order solvers remain unstable under strong external guidance (the long-standing DPM-Solver++ warning still applies), so pair with modest guidance and stochastic churn. Note flow-matching-side **Manifold-Projection CFG (2601.21892, Jan 2026 — verified in-window)** = "Improving Classifier-Free Guidance of Flow Matching via Manifold Projection" (Cai, Liu, Su, Wang; homotopy optimization + Anderson Acceleration) is the analogous ODE-fix but flow-only, less applicable to ADM.

**Honest read on hype (post-verification):** the noise-search cluster is substance — it reliably converts compute into CLIP-reward and is aesthetic-safe, and IPR ships confirmed code (github.com/ahn-ml/IPR); Trust-Region's code claim, however, could **not** be verified. The attention-guidance successors (GAG, Momentum, C²FG) are plausible but code-less preprints validated mostly on latent DiTs/flow models (FLUX, SD3), not pixel ADM — treat as experiments. Flash-BoN and ZeNO are the freshest and least reproduced. Two anchors — **TPG (June 2025)** and **S²-Guidance (Aug 2025)** — technically *predate* this Sept-2025+ sweep and are retained only as the mature/sibling reference points, not as new-in-window results; the Feynman-Kac framework (Jan 2025) is the foundational predecessor, not part of the window.

Since the objectives already covered (VSD/ProlificDreamer, CSD, NFSD, ISM, SDI, ASD, SDS-Bridge, DMD/DMD2, SIM), the field has bifurcated into two lines that both matter for Notebook B's SDS-grad block: (a) **score-identity / distribution-matching objectives** that replace the noisy `(eps_pred - eps)` SDS residual with a lower-variance, mode-covering gradient, and (b) **reward/negative-prompt-aware distillation** that fixes the oversaturation-vs-shape trade-off directly. The single biggest shift in this window is that the SiD/DMD "distill-a-generator" machinery is now being used *as the optimization prior itself*, not just to make fast samplers. Below, "the SDS residual" means the gradient `w(t)(eps_θ(x_t,t,y) - eps)` that Notebook B currently back-props through diffvg/Bezier-Splatting from the 512 UNet under DreamTime anneal. *All citations below verified in-window as of 2026-07-20.*

**Score-identity as the prior (the strongest lever).** *Score Distillation of Flow Matching Models* (arXiv 2509.25127, submitted 29 Sep 2025, rev. Dec 2025; code at github.com/apple/ml-sid-dit — informally "SiD-DiT"; note the paper title itself does **not** carry the "SiD-DiT" tag, and the repo ships training/generation code but published distilled weights are **not confirmed** on the repo page) gives a clean Bayes-rule derivation unifying Gaussian diffusion and flow-matching, then ports **Score identity Distillation (SiD)** — whose gradient is the model-vs-fake **score difference** rather than the score-vs-noise residual — onto SANA/SD3-Medium/SD3.5-Medium/Large/FLUX.1-dev DiTs, data-free (verified: authors state this is "the first systematic evidence that score distillation applies broadly to text-to-image flow matching models"). The relevance to us is the *objective*: SiD's score-difference gradient is markedly lower-variance than SDS and does not require the CFG over-boosting that causes disco→neon oversaturation. This is the natural successor to SDS-Bridge/ISM for the vector loop. Its 2026 refinement — *SGMD* (arXiv 2605.30116, submitted 28 May 2026; venue "ICML 2026" claimed in draft is **unverified**) — targets the two-timescale lag between the fake-score net and the moving generator, optimizing the fake score toward the teacher with a teacher stop-gradient Fisher distribution-matching objective plus dual-potential techniques, giving a ~3× cheaper (vs DMD2), more stable score-matching gradient that preserves motion/detail (i.e., less mode collapse of fine primitives). *Caveat:* the draft's exact "negative-residual (outer) + residual-contraction (inner)" phrasing is a plausible reading of the paper's "dual potential" but is not verbatim from the abstract. SGMD code is attributed to github.com/ModelTC/LightX2V — the repo **exists** (2.5k stars, real video/image inference framework, author overlap with SGMD/Phased-DMD teams) but its README does **not** mention SGMD by name, so treat the exact code location as **unconfirmed**.

**Distribution-matching as the prior.** *Phased DMD* (arXiv 2510.27684, submitted 31 Oct 2025; project x-niper.github.io/projects/Phased-DMD) breaks DMD2's diversity collapse by splitting the SNR axis into subintervals and doing progressive distribution-matching + score-matching per phase with a MoE — a multi-step DMD that keeps diversity, which maps onto a *coarse-to-fine DreamTime schedule* over primitives (verified: validated on image + video generators, cites motion-dynamics and image-diversity gains). *DMD Meets RL / DMDR* (arXiv 2511.13649, submitted 17 Nov 2025) shows reward-tilted distribution matching where **DMD acts as a regularizer against reward-hacking** while RL supplies preference-awareness (verified: "Reward-Tilted Distribution Matching" + two dynamic distillation strategies) — directly usable if we want CLIP/aesthetic reward pressure on the vector loop without the palette blowing out. *rCM* (Score-Regularized Continuous-Time Consistency, arXiv 2510.08431, submitted 9 Oct 2025, ICLR 2026; github.com/NVlabs/rcm, official, ~767 stars) folds **score distillation in as a long-skip regularizer** on a consistency model, explicitly blending forward-divergence (mode-covering) + reverse-KL (mode-seeking) terms; it scales to 10–14B (verified) and is the cleanest recipe for a distilled few-step teacher whose gradient you can query cheaply during optimization.

**Fixing oversaturation/over-smoothing directly.** Three 2025-H2/2026 papers attack the exact disco-killing artifacts. *Target-Balanced Score Distillation / TBSD* (arXiv 2511.11710, submitted 12 Nov 2025) reframes the texture-vs-shape tension as **multi-objective optimization over Target Negative Prompts**, adaptively weighting the negative branch so you get texture without shape distortion (verified: TNPs "enhance texture realism… but induce shape distortions," solved via adaptive multi-objective strategy) — a near-drop-in replacement for our CFG/negative term. *AnchorDS* (arXiv 2511.11692, submitted 12 Nov 2025; "AAAI 2026" **unverified**) treats the source distribution as *dynamically evolving* and adds **image-conditioned state-anchored guidance** with a penalty on wrong source estimates, killing "semantic over-smoothing" (verified) — attractive because our render is already an image we can condition on. *Preference Score Distillation / PSD* (arXiv 2603.01594, submitted 2 Mar 2026; full title "Preference Score Distillation: Leveraging 2D Rewards to Align Text-to-3D Generation with Human Preference") routes a 2D reward model **through classifier-free-guidance theory as an implicit reward score** (instead of adding raw reward gradients that corrupt denoising) with adaptive co-optimization of the negative embedding (verified) — the principled version of "reward-weighted SDS."

**Honest maturity read.** SiD-DiT and rCM are the substantive, code-released items and worth a real port (weights availability for ml-sid-dit unconfirmed; rCM ships checkpoints); SGMD/Phased-DMD/DMDR have code but are tuned for video/T2I one-step sampling, so adapting the gradient to a diffvg render is a research-bet. TBSD/AnchorDS/PSD are preprint-only (project pages, releases unconfirmed) but are cheap conceptual retrofits to our existing negative-prompt/reward terms. **Caveat on the "fast-sampling" cluster** — 1.x-Distill (arXiv 2604.04018, submitted 5 Apr 2026, *verified*: fractional-step DMD, distribution-matching + adversarial refinement + caching on SD variants), Mean Flow Distillation (arXiv 2606.11155, submitted 9 Jun 2026, *verified*: temporal low-pass filter for flow-matching, single-step), Flow Map Distillation Without Data (arXiv 2511.19428, submitted 24 Nov 2025, *verified*: data-free flow-map distillation, FID 1.45 on ImageNet-256 @ 1 step from SiT-XL/2+REPA) — these optimize *inference speed*, not an optimization prior; they only help us indirectly by yielding a cheaper teacher to query, and most target flow-matching backbones we are not using in Notebook A/B. (The draft's parenthetical "Continuous-Time Distribution Matching, 2605.06376" was not in the citation set and was not verified.) Aesthetically, SiD/DMD-style score-difference gradients are lower-variance and less CFG-dependent than SDS, so they *help* preserve the soft painterly look rather than sharpening it toward SD.

## State of the field, Sept 2025 → mid-2026

This niche did **not** slow down. Since Bézier Splatting (NeurIPS 2025, arXiv 2503.16424 — already the notebook's `splat` renderer) the action split into three fronts: (a) **new differentiable rasterizers** that attack diffvg/Bézier-Splatting on stability rather than raw speed; (b) **native/AR SVG generators** that have converged on *reasoning + RL (GRPO)* as the dominant recipe, displacing pure SDS-over-diffvg for icon/diagram-scale work; (c) a genuinely new **SVG-animation** subfield. Honest caveat: almost everything below is **preprint-only with no released code as of this fetch** — the 2026 SVG literature is running well ahead of its reproductions.

*Verification note (fact-check pass, 2026-07-20): all 13 arXiv IDs below were fetched directly and confirmed to resolve to the cited paper with matching title/abstract. A deliberate control fetch of a non-existent ID returned HTTP 404, confirming the fetch pipeline distinguishes real from fake IDs. Every item is in the recency window (arXiv ≥2025-09) except the Bézier-Splatting baseline (Mar 2025), which is the intentional pre-window reference.*

### Differentiable rasterizers (map to `bezier_splat_canvas.py` / the `renderer` switch)

- **NURBS Splatting** (arXiv **2606.31764**, Jun 2026; Qiu & Zhou, Hunan Univ.). *[Confirmed, in-window.]* Represents planar **rational** curves as continuous Gaussian fields, so long splines, non-uniform knots, rational weights and closed-region fills all render with stable gradients — explicitly pitched against "Bézier + analytic rasterization" gradient instability. This is the same lineage as the "NURBS splatting" already in your COVERED list; treat 2606.31764 as its dated, current arXiv form. Concretely it's a **drop-in successor to `BezierSplatCanvas`**: your shapes are two-cubic-half closed Béziers, a strict subset of what a NURBS-field rasterizer covers, and it would let a single tier carry longer, gradient-fill-friendly strokes without the analytic boundary-sampling diffvg needs. Preserves the soft/painterly look (Gaussian accumulation is inherently smooth). *No code.*
- **Vector Scaffolding** (arXiv **2605.11913**, May 2026, v2 Jun 2026; Lee et al., camera-ready ECCV 2026). *[Confirmed, in-window.]* Diagnoses "topology collapse" as an **area-vs-boundary gradient imbalance** and fixes it with *Interior Gradient Aggregation* + *Progressive Stratification* (+ Rapid Inflation Scheduling): 2.5× faster and up to **+1.4 dB** PSNR at identical curve budget over Bézier-Splatting-style optimization, no "polygon soup." This is an **optimizer/schedule upgrade to your warm-start + tier `path_schedule`**, not a renderer swap — highly compatible with blue-noise tier placement. *No code.*
- **Neural Image Abstraction Using Long Smoothing B-Splines** (arXiv **2511.05360**, Nov 2025; Berio, Calinon, Leymarie, Deussen, Shamir et al.). *[Confirmed, in-window.]* Injects **smoothing B-splines into diffvg via a linear map**, with a derivative-based smoothing cost trading fidelity↔simplicity; demos include stroke-based abstraction and closed-area abstraction. This is a **new `add_path_tier` primitive**: long, clean painterly strokes instead of many short cubics — a strong aesthetic match for the "disco/painterly" target and for your divisionism/SBR modes. *No code.*
- **Optimizing 4D Wires for Sparse 3D Abstraction** (arXiv **2605.11977**, May 2026; Wu & Lee). *[Confirmed, in-window.]* Mostly 3D (single continuous 4D wire as an (x,y,z,w) B-spline), but ships a **differentiable variable-width curve renderer** and reframes stroke placement as *global routing* rather than local density accumulation — a portable idea for your tier scheduler if you want single continuous variable-width strokes. Supports SDS or CLIP optimization. Research-bet relevance. *No code.*

### Native / autoregressive SVG generators (warm-start & native export)

- **DuetSVG** (arXiv **2512.10894**, Dec 2025; Zhang, Zhao, Fisher, Xu, Liao, Liu — Adobe/CityU). *[Confirmed, in-window.]* Jointly emits **image tokens and SVG tokens** end-to-end and adds a **test-time-scaling** step using the model's own visual predictions as internal guidance — an OmniSVG/StarVector successor addressing geometric coherence. Best used as a **warm-start SVG provider** to seed your tiers before CLIP+SDS refinement. *No code confirmed.*
- **SVGThinker** (arXiv **2509.24299**, Sep 2025; Chen et al., SJTU). *[Confirmed, in-window — earliest item, right at the window edge.]* **Reasoning-driven text-to-SVG**: renders each primitive in sequence, annotates code+image with a multimodal model, SFT on exposed reasoning. Outputs editable SVG directly — again a **warm-start seed**. *No code confirmed.*
- **CTRL-S / "Reliable Reasoning in SVG-LLMs"** (arXiv **2603.16189**, Mar 2026; Wang et al.) and **GeoSVG-RL** (arXiv **2605.25447**, May 2026; Li, Cai, Chen, Wang). *[Both confirmed, in-window.]* The field has standardized on **GRPO with hybrid rewards** (DINO + image-text sim + format + code-efficiency for CTRL-S, which ships the 145K SVG-Sophia dataset; explicit geometric/layout constraints + a browser-backed 6-dimension verifier for GeoSVG-RL). Relevant to your **reward-ranking** stage more than the differentiable loop; GeoSVG-RL is diagram-specific and lowest-priority for a painterly notebook. *No code.*

### New subfield: SVG animation (maps to your LiveSketch vector-animation layer)

- **LiveSVG** (arXiv **2605.30174**, May 2026; Levy, Samuel, Pritch, Peleg, Shamir, Lischinski et al.). *[Confirmed, in-window.]* **Zero-shot**: generate a preview video with a frozen I2V model, then **fit the SVG to it via differentiable rendering** using a *dual-level motion model* — per-group homographies (coarse articulation) + per-path Bézier control-point offsets (local deformation), plus sphere-packing recolorization; ships a ChallengeSVG benchmark. This is a cleaner successor to the covered **LiveSketch** (affine+residual) and plugs straight into your diffvg/splat fitting path for fully-editable output. *No code confirmed.*
- **VAnim** (arXiv **2605.01517**, May 2026; Liang et al., accepted ICML 2026). *[Confirmed, in-window.]* First **LLM text-to-SVG-animation** framework; models animation as **Sparse State Updates on a persistent SVG DOM** (≈9.8× sequence compression) with rendering-aware GRPO; ships the SVGAnim-134k benchmark. Orthogonal (code-space, not gradient-space) but interesting for keyframe scaffolding. *No code.*

### Raster↔vector bridge
- **"Controlling Your Image via Simplified Vector Graphics"** (arXiv **2602.14443**, Feb 2026; Guo, Liu, Wang, Li, Huang). *[Confirmed, in-window. Note: the draft's "Vec2Pix" nickname is not the paper's official name — cite by full title.]* Parses an image into a **hierarchical simplified-VG layer**, edits it (shape/color/add-remove), and re-synthesizes photorealistically — a two-way bridge that could couple your raster "disco" notebook (A) and vector notebook (B): vectorize a disco frame, edit shapes, regenerate. *Project page only.*

**Benchmark:** **VectorGym** (multitask SVG code-gen/sketching/editing, arXiv **2603.29852**, ~Feb–Mar 2026; Rodriguez et al.) — *now verified: real and in-window (independently mirrored on HuggingFace Papers and OpenReview). Four tasks (Sketch2SVG, VG-Edit, Text2SVG, VG-Cap) with human-authored annotations + a GRPO/curriculum multi-task RL baseline on Qwen3-VL 8B.* Upgrade from the draft's "TO-VERIFY" status to a citable benchmark. (Minor: WebFetch/WebSearch report an announcement date of ~Feb 22, 2026 despite the 2603 ID — immaterial to recency; well inside the window either way.)

*Reference baseline (COVERED, pre-window):* **Bézier Splatting** (arXiv 2503.16424, Mar 2025, NeurIPS 2025; Liu, Zhou, Zhao, Huang) — confirmed, correctly dated; 30×/150× faster forward/backward vs diffvg. Kept as the existing `splat` renderer, not a recency claim.

## What actually moved in this window

The honest headline: **new drop-in CLIP-style text-image encoders have gone quiet since H1-2025.** After SigLIP2, MetaCLIP2 (2507.22062 — "Meta CLIP 2: A Worldwide Scaling Recipe", *Jul 2025, predates the Sep-window but already in our survey as context*), Perception Encoder and AIMv2, the Sept-2025→2026 encoder frontier *pivoted away* from the contrastive image↔text tower that our guidance actually needs. The two biggest releases are both wrong-shaped for CLIP guidance: **OpenVision 2** (arXiv 2509.01644, "A Family of Generative Pretrained Visual Encoders for Multimodal Learning", Sep 2025, **CVPR 2026**; v3 released Jan 2026, code at `UCSC-VLAA/OpenVision`) deliberately *removes the text encoder and the contrastive loss*, retaining only the captioning loss as a purely generative signal (verified ~1.5× faster train — 83h→57h on ViT-L/14, ~1.8× less memory, scales >1B) — great for feeding an MLLM, useless as a `text_embed·image_embed` scorer. **DINOv3** (2508.10104, *Aug 2025 — sits ~3 weeks before the Sep cutoff*; weights on `facebookresearch/dinov3`, ViT-S through ViT-7B + ConvNeXt) is pure SSL with Gram-anchoring to stop dense-feature degradation over long schedules — no text tower (a `dino.txt` alignment variant exists only as an optional downstream add-on), so it belongs in our *perceptual/structure* loss slot (a DINO-feature or DreamSim-style term), not `cond_fn`. It is kept here because it supersedes our DINOv2-era perceptual features. The only in-window contrastive tweak that is genuinely new is **β-CLIP** (2512.12678, "Text-Conditioned Contrastive Learning for Multi-Granular Vision-Language Alignment", Dec 2025) — hierarchical caption→sentence→phrase→region alignment via cross-attention and a β-CAL loss (SOTA among no-hard-negative methods: 91.8% T2I / 92.3% I2T R@1 on Urban1K), which could sharpen phrase-level steering in the ensemble but is preprint-only and unproven at our scale. **Net:** for the CLIP-load cell, keep the SigLIP2/PE/MetaCLIP2 ensemble; there is no compelling *newer* open contrastive encoder to add. Report this as a real field observation, not a gap in my search.

## Where the action is: reward / selector models

The energy shifted entirely to **VLM-based preference/reward models**, which is exactly our *ranker / best-of-N selector* module (post-`do_run` in Notebook A; the SDS+CLIP objective and final-candidate pick in Notebook B).

- **HPSv3 / HPSv3++** — the clear successor to the HPSv2 already in our survey (this supersession is why HPSv3 stays despite its Aug-2025 arXiv date sitting just before the strict Sep window). HPSv3 (2508.03789, "Towards Wide-Spectrum Human Preference Score", Aug 2025, **ICCV 2025**, **code+weights released** — `MizzenAI/HPSv3`, project page `mizzenai.github.io/HPSv3.project`) is a VLM-based reward *(the specific Qwen2-VL backbone claim is not stated on the abstract or project page — treat as unverified)* trained on **HPDv3** (1.08M text-image pairs, 1.17M pairwise comparisons) with an *uncertainty-aware ranking loss*, plus **CoHP** (Chain-of-Human-Preference) — an iterative image-refinement loop that is a drop-in recipe for our render→rank→reseed loop. **HPSv3++** (2606.14657, "Scaling Reward Models Across the Full Spectrum of Diffusion Model Capabilities", Jun 2026, code released — `PlantPotatoOnMoon/HPSv3-PlusPlus`, weights `Junjun2333/HPSv3-PlusPlus`) improves it via a two-stage framework: Stage 1 *data-aware orthogonal-gradient projection* to absorb new aesthetic preferences without forgetting, Stage 2 capability-iteration conditioning on unlabeled data; it reports gains on HPDv3++ (~191–212K pairs) and GenAI-Bench *(exact +9.8%/+5.5% figures in the draft could not be confirmed from the abstract — treat as approximate)*. This is the single highest-value swap for our selector.
- **UnifiedReward-2.0 / -Think / -Flex** (`CodeGoat24/UnifiedReward`, MIT; base model UnifiedReward "Unified Reward Model for Multimodal Understanding and Generation", 2503.05236, *Mar 2025 — the base paper predates the window; the in-window artifacts are the numbered releases below*). Verified from the repo: **-2.0** (Sep 25 2025, Qwen/Qwen3-VL 3B–72B), **-Think** (NeurIPS 2025, released Nov 17 2025 — "first unified multimodal CoT reward model", 2B–32B), **-Flex** (released **Feb 1 2026** — draft said "Mar 2026"; corrected — "unified personalized reward model for vision generation"). One model gives both pairwise ranking *and* pointwise scores for image+video; -Think adds CoT reasoning, -Flex adds context-adaptive/personalized reasoning. Strong general selector; heavier (7B+) than HPSv3.
- **OmniQuality-R** (2510.10609, "Advancing Reward Models Through All-Encompassing Quality Assessment", Oct 2025) decomposes the score into *aesthetic / technical / text-alignment* dimensions via GRPO with a Gaussian continuous-score reward (plus std-filtering and entropy gating for stability). The dimensional split matters for us: we can reward-guide on **aesthetics + alignment while ignoring "technical" sharpness**, which is precisely how you avoid dragging the disco look toward clean photoreal.
- **RewardDance** (2509.08826, "Reward Scaling in Visual Generation", Sep 2025, ByteDance Seed) reformulates reward as the VLM's P("yes" it beats a reference), scaling to 26B with demonstrated *resistance to reward-hacking* (reward variance stays high during RL fine-tuning) — relevant if we ever do reward-guided sampling rather than just best-of-N.
- **ArtiMuse** (2507.14533, "Fine-Grained Image Aesthetics Assessment with Joint Scoring and Expert-Level Understanding", *arXiv Jul 2025 — predates the strict window, but accepted to **CVPR 2026** and kept for its unique painterly value*; **weights released** — `thunderbolt215/ArtiMuse`, HF+ModelScope) is built on **InternVL-3** for *fine-grained image-aesthetics* with 8-attribute expert scoring on the art-curated ArtiMuse-10K dataset (10K images, 5 categories / 15 subcategories) — the most painterly-sympathetic selector here, and the best candidate for ranking our dreamlike outputs on composition/color-harmony rather than realism.

## The photoreal-bias caveat (critical for our aesthetic)

Every human-preference reward above is trained on modern T2I outputs, so best-of-N against them **pulls toward polished, high-contrast, anime/photoreal "SDXL-pretty."** Two mitigations: (1) prefer **ArtiMuse / OmniQuality-R's aesthetic-only axis** over raw HPS, and (2) use the reward strictly as a *selector across seeds*, never as an in-loop `cond_fn` gradient, so it re-ranks rather than reshapes the sampler. For evaluation only, **Visual Aesthetic Benchmark** (2605.12684, "Can Frontier Models Judge Beauty?", May 2026 — 400 tasks / 1,195 images across fine art, photography, illustration; best of 20 frontier models hit only 26.5% vs 68.9% human) and **RAPO / Aes-R1** (2509.21871, "Unlocking the Essence of Beauty: Advanced Aesthetic Reasoning with Relative-Absolute Policy Optimization", Sep 2025 — RAPO is the RL algorithm, Aes-R1 the model) are useful to sanity-check whether a reward even correlates with *artistic* beauty. VQAScore (2404.01291) — named in the brief — is pre-window (Apr 2024) and alignment-only; treat HPSv3++/UnifiedReward-Think as its de-facto successors for our purposes.

**Bottom line:** don't touch the encoder ensemble; do add **HPSv3++ (or HPSv3+CoHP) as the primary best-of-N selector**, with **ArtiMuse** as the painterly-aware tiebreaker and **OmniQuality-R** if we want axis-selective rewarding.

## Landscape shift in the window

Two things changed materially since our Part II. First, the **motion prior itself was replaced**: the aging AnimateDiff/ModelScope/SVD backbones that LiveSketch and Dynamic Typography distilled through are now obsolete. The open motion-SDS prior of 2026 is a **DiT flow-matching video model** — **Wan 2.2** (Apache-2.0, a two-expert MoE ~14B/expert I2V + a 5B TI2V that runs on a single 4090) and **LTX-2.x** (LTX-2 line from Lightricks; the specific "2.3" point release is *unverified*) — and, critically, distilled **4-step Lightning LoRAs** (Wan2.2-Lightning, ~20x speed-up, no CFG) that make a full video sample cheap enough to sit inside an optimization loop. Second, **vector animation moved away from raw SDS**. The LiveSketch recipe (distill a noisy per-coordinate displacement gradient) is being superseded by a **"generate-then-fit"** paradigm (LiveSVG) and by **implicit-representation bridges** (LINR) that tame SDS variance. This is the single most relevant development for our Notebook B N10 module.

## Vector-animation successors to LiveSketch (Notebook B)

- **LiveSVG (arXiv:2605.30174, May 2026)** is the cleanest LiveSketch beat. Instead of optimizing Bézier control points against a jittery SDS gradient, it renders a **preview video from a frozen I2V model**, then fits the *original* diffvg paths to that video by differentiable rendering. Motion is a **dual-level representation: per-group homographies/transforms (coarse articulation) + per-path Bézier offsets (local deformation)** — structurally identical to our affine+residual module, so it drops into the N10 loop as a swap of the *loss target* (a concrete video) for the SDS score. A recolorization trick resolves pixel-correspondence ambiguity, and the authors ship a **ChallengeSVG** benchmark. Preserves the vector aesthetic because we still optimize *our* primitives. Preprint (Levy et al.); project/benchmark described, code release not yet confirmed.
- **LINR Bridge (arXiv:2509.07484, Sept 2025)** keeps VSDS but inserts a **layered implicit neural representation** as an intermediate to bridge the SVG↔raster domain gap, then warps it; a lower-variance drop-in for our existing video-SDS path. Preprint (Gao, Lan, Zhang, Yang).
- **GroupSketch (arXiv:2508.15535, Aug 2025, ACM MM 2025)** and **Vector Prism (arXiv:2512.14336, Dec 2025)** both attack the *"which primitives move together"* problem our flat diffvg path list ignores: GroupSketch runs a two-stage Motion Initialization → Refinement pipeline with a **Group-based Displacement Network** for multi-object temporal consistency; Vector Prism recovers semantic groups by **statistical aggregation of weak part predictions** so a VLM can drive coherent motion. Use either as a **pre-pass** that clusters our paths before the affine module. Preprints. (GroupSketch's arXiv timestamp is Aug 21 2025 — a hair before a strict Sept cutoff but squarely inside the 2025H2 window and the ACM MM 2025 cycle.)
- **VideoSketcher (arXiv:2602.15819, Feb 2026)** pairs an LLM **semantic planner** with a video-diffusion **renderer** to emit temporally coherent *raster* sketch sequences (stroke ordering from synthetic shape compositions; style distilled from as few as seven hand-drawn examples) — usable as a fitting target or stroke-order prior. **Sketch2Motion** (arXiv:2605.28394; correct full title *"Text-driven 2D Sketch to 3D Animation via Diffusion-guided Skeleton Optimization"*, May 2026) and **Animus3D (SIGGRAPH Asia 2025, arXiv:2512.12534, Dec 2025)** introduce **MoSDS/MSD**, motion-aware SDS variants that explicitly penalize the "minimal-motion + jitter" failure of vanilla SDS — worth porting into our vector loss even though both target 3D skeletons/meshes.

## Temporal consistency for the raster per-frame sampler (Notebook A)

Our `do_run`/`cond_fn` sampler hallucinates each frame independently and warps with optical flow. Two in-window ideas upgrade that:

- **Motion Prior Distillation / MPD (ICLR 2026, github vvsjeon/MPDsampler; project page vvsjeon.github.io/MPD)** is inference-time **generative inbetweening**: it distills the *motion residual of the forward path into the backward path* to kill the bidirectional mismatch (each path otherwise follows the motion prior of its own conditioning frame) that makes off-the-shelf I2V inbetweening flicker. Applies to off-the-shelf inbetweening models with **no parameter changes**. Use it to interpolate between two disco keyframes with an off-the-shelf I2V model, then run CLIP guidance per interpolated frame — preserves per-frame disco because the video model only supplies *coherent motion*, not final pixels. Code released (built on SVD-XT / Stability generative-models).
- **UniCaMo (arXiv:2607.02798, July 2026** — full title *"Track the Noise, Move the World: 3D-Grounded Motion-Consistent Noise for Controllable Video Generation"*) and its lineage (**Go-with-the-Flow**, CVPR 2025, arXiv:2501.08331 — **predates window**, the baseline UniCaMo beats) do **structured noise warping**: warp the Gaussian noise along sparse 3D point tracks (+ a spherical noise field for camera moves / newly-revealed regions). This replaces our naïve pixel-warp with *noise-space* coherence, so the sampler's stochastic hallucination stays but is temporally correlated — exactly the "keep the dream, kill the flicker" tradeoff we want for 3D/turbo animation. Lightweight LoRA on Wan (2.1); preprint.
- **Training-free motion transfer** — **FlowMotion (CVPR 2026, arXiv:2603.06289, March 2026; github HKUST-LongGroup/FlowMotion)** and **HALO (ECCV 2026, sunyj-hxppy.github.io/halo)** — extract a motion signal from a reference video (early-latent flow-prediction alignment + velocity regularization; motion-specialized attention heads respectively) and inject it at inference with no retraining. Feeds a vector *or* raster loop as a way to script camera/object motion into the prior. Both have released/linked code; FlowMotion runs on a single RTX 3090 (~5–20 GB with offloading).

*(Bibliographic note: **Causal-Forcing / Causal-Forcing++**, ICML 2026, github thu-ml/Causal-Forcing — AR diffusion distillation for 1/2/4-step real-time interactive video — is a confirmed, in-window repo in our citation set but is **not yet wired into a module** in this section; retained for the real-time/turbo bibliography.)*

## Honest read

Wan 2.2 + Lightning and MPD/FlowMotion are **real and reproducible today** (Wan/Lightning shipped mid-2025 and remain the current backbone). LiveSVG is the highest-impact idea for us but is a fresh preprint (project page + ChallengeSVG benchmark, code release unconfirmed) — treat the mechanism as the deliverable, not a drop-in. The 3D MoSDS/MSD papers are over-claimed for our 2D case but the anti-jitter objective is genuine. The subfield did *not* go quiet; if anything vector animation is unusually active this cycle (see the Oct 2025 Sketch-Animation STAR, arXiv:2510.10218, Rai & Sharma).

## What actually moved in this window

Two subfields shifted meaningfully since Sept 2025, both directly relevant to our vector tier stack and to a differentiable raster prior for `cond_fn`.

**(1) Differentiable soft-tessellation became a real image-representation primitive.** The standout is **Soft Anisotropic Diagrams (SAD)** (arXiv:2604.21984, Apr 2026 — confirmed). It represents an image as a set of adaptive *sites* carrying learned anisotropic metrics; each pixel color is a **softmax blend over a per-pixel top-K nearest sites** — i.e. a fully differentiable soft anisotropic-power-Voronoi partition, GPU-local, no diffvg-style global rasterization. Reported 46.0 dB PSNR on Kodak at 2.2 s encode and 4–19× training speedups over state-of-the-art baselines (Image-GS class). This is the single most important item for us: it is a *new vector primitive tier*. Our `bezier_splat_canvas.add_tier` (bezier_splat_canvas.py:74) speaks Bezier-splat 2D Gaussians; SAD gives a companion **anisotropic-cell / stained-glass tier** whose soft-argmax cells produce the flat-facet mosaic look diffvg can't do cheaply, while staying end-to-end differentiable for CLIP+SDS guidance. It supersedes classic (non-differentiable) Voronoi stained-glass mosaicing and pairs naturally with our divisionism mode. Preprint-only, no code released yet — but the math is simple enough to reimplement (top-K softmax over site metrics). Effort medium, impact high.

**(2) Neural Cellular Automata generalized off the grid and up in resolution.** The substrate **"NCA: From Cells to Pixels"** (arXiv:2506.22899, Jun 2025, SIGGRAPH 2026) coupled a coarse-grid NCA with a lightweight **implicit decoder** (cell-state + local-coord → color) to render arbitrary/HD resolution in real time — this is the enabling trick, but note it **(predates window)**: it landed June 2025, before our Sept-2025 cutoff, and is included as the foundation the in-window successors build on. **Its official code and pretrained models are released** (github.com/TheDevilWillBeBee/Cells2Pixels — train.py + configs + Colab), so this one is clone-a-repo, not reimplement-from-paper. Two 2026 successors build on it: **Neural Particle Automata (NPA)** (arXiv:2601.16096, Jan 2026, SIGGRAPH 2026) makes each cell a **particle with continuous position + internal state**, updated by a shared neural rule over dynamic neighborhoods via **differentiable SPH operators** — a Lagrangian NCA, retaining regeneration/robustness across morphogenesis, point-cloud (PointMNIST classification), and texture-synthesis tasks; and **Genome-Driven NCA** (arXiv:2605.13630, May 2026) adds precise **genome-channel initialization** enabling *texture grafting at inference without retraining* plus robust self-repair. For us these are best used as a **`cond_fn` auxiliary texture prior** and as an **init/texture generator**: a pre-trained NCA/NPA producing a self-organizing painterly substrate (organic, non-tileable, dreamlike) that seeds Notebook B tiers or biases the disco raster loss toward emergent texture rather than sharp SD detail — strongly aesthetic-preserving. Maturity correction: **NPA has released official code + two pretrained checkpoints** (github.com/TheDevilWillBeBee/NPA — lizard.pth / polka_dotted.pth, CUDA/nanobind kernels), so the particle plumbing is available to clone, not a research-bet-from-scratch; Genome-NCA is medium effort (paper-only). The µNCA lineage means these run in a few hundred parameters, so an aux term is cheap.

**(3) Differentiable stroke-based rendering had a genuine refresh — the successor generation to Paint Transformer / Stylized Neural Painting.** **"Birth of a Painting" (DiffPaint)** (arXiv:2511.13191, Nov 2025 — confirmed) unifies painting, stylized texturing, and *smudging* in one differentiable pipeline: Bezier strokes through a differentiable paint renderer **plus a differentiable smudge operator** for natural color blending — coarse-to-fine, tested on oil/watercolor/ink/digital. The smudge operator is the new idea and is exactly the soft painterly blending our "disco" look wants; it maps to a new painterly stroke tier in `add_tier` and a render-time smudge op. Complementing it, **Differentiable Stroke Planning with Dual Parameterization** (arXiv:2604.02752, Apr 2026 — confirmed) couples **discrete polylines ↔ continuous Bezier control points** via a bidirectional map so gradient refinement escapes the local minima that trap pure-differentiable SBR, cutting stroke counts 30–50% and time 30–40% with GS-style parallel optimization — a drop-in efficiency upgrade for our stroke tier optimizer. Both preprint-only; the DiffPaint project page currently lists no code (treat as reimplement). Birth-of-a-Painting: impact high; Dual-Param: medium.

**(4) Layered image→vector warm-start improved.** **AmodalSVG** (arXiv:2604.10940, Apr 2026 — confirmed) does **amodal vectorization via semantic layer peeling** (progressive layer decomposition + inpainting of occluded regions) then **adaptive layered vectorization**. It supersedes LayerPeeler/LayerTracer for our init pipeline by yielding occlusion-complete, editable layers — a better SLIC/LIVE-style warm-start for Notebook B. (Note: the abstract confirms semantic layer peeling + adaptive layered vectorization; the "VLM-driven / error-budget-driven" specifics are from the method body, not the abstract — sound but verify against the full paper before quoting.) Also newer on the 2D-Gaussian-image front: **SGI: Structured 2D Gaussians** (arXiv:2603.07789, Mar 2026 — confirmed) is a more compact successor to GaussianImage/Image-GS for large-image init (up to 7.5× compression, 1.6–6.5× faster optimization). **InkDiffuser** (arXiv:2605.05865, May 2026 — confirmed) offers one-shot Chinese calligraphy via a differentiable *morphological* ink-structure loss (DIS) inside a diffusion framework — a niche ink-stroke tier. On procedural texture/material: **"Toward Richer Material Generation via Procedural Data Enhancement"** (arXiv:2606.14988, Jun 2026; draft labels it SIGGRAPH 2026 — venue not independently confirmed) augments single-lobe PBR into multi-lobe neural-latent materials — tangential to us (3D/PBR-leaning), low priority.

## Niches that stayed quiet

- **Differentiable halftone / dithering / stippling**: quiet — nothing new verifiable in-window; the most recent relevant work remains DRL halftoning (arXiv:2304.12152, **Apr 2023** — the draft's "2024" was wrong; corrected). A differentiable ordered-dither tier is still an open gap.
- **Aperiodic-monotile *differentiable/generative* tiling**: quiet on the ML side. Only pure-math follow-ups to the Hat/Spectre exist (e.g. homochiral inflation, arXiv:2502.15608, **Feb 2025 — predates window**, kept only as evidence of the gap); no differentiable-tiling or diffusion-over-monotiles result exists yet. Genuine research gap, not a missed paper.

## State of the window

Two honest headlines first. (1) **Classic color-appearance science went quiet.** No new CAM16-successor, no fresh differentiable Kubelka-Munk / spectral-mixing result of note landed between Sep 2025 and Jul 2026 — the color action migrated wholesale into **optimal-transport color matching**, where the news is real. ## Color / OT — the live subfield

**ReSWD (arXiv:2510.01061, "ReSWD: ReSTIR'd, not shaken. Combining Reservoir Sampling and Sliced Wasserstein Distance for Variance Reduction", Boss/Engelhardt/Donné/Jampani, 1 Oct 2025, Stability AI; code: github.com/Stability-AI/ReSWD — both verified in-window)** is the single most plug-in-ready item for either notebook. The math: the sliced-Wasserstein Monte-Carlo estimator averages 1-D Wasserstein distances over *random* projection directions, which produces **noisy gradients**. ReSWD folds **Weighted Reservoir Sampling** into SWD to *adaptively retain the informative projection directions* across optimization steps while staying unbiased — lower-variance gradients at the same slice budget. It supersedes the vanilla SWD used in **SW-Guidance ("Color Conditional Generation with Sliced-Wasserstein Guidance", Lobashev/Larchenko/Guskov, arXiv:2503.19034 — VERIFIED correct ID, but note the arXiv predates the window at Mar 2025 (v2 May 2026); the NeurIPS 2025 spotlight venue is in-window; code: github.com/alobashev/sw-guidance, verified official)**, the training-free diffusion-sampling scheme that adds a differentiable Sliced-1-Wasserstein term between generated- and reference-palette color distributions. SW-Guidance is retained here as the baseline ReSWD supersedes, not as a fresh-window discovery. Plug-in sites: (a) Notebook A — drop the ReSWD-estimated SW color distance straight into `cond_fn` as an extra palette-guidance gradient, preserving the soft disco look because it steers *color statistics*, not structure; (b) either notebook — replace any sliced-Wasserstein feature/style loss (your CX/sliced-Wasserstein-feature anchor) with the reservoir estimator for cleaner descent; (c) Notebook B — use it as the `palette`/learned-palette OT objective. Maturity: code released (official Stability-AI repo, project page reservoirswd.github.io, HF demo), demonstrated on color correction *and* diffusion guidance. High impact, quick-to-medium effort.

A second, palette-native color item verified in-window: **Palette Aligned Image Diffusion (arXiv:2509.02000, Aharoni/Porat/Lischinski/Shamir, 2 Sep 2025)** — the method is named **Palette-Adapter** (the paper title is "Palette Aligned Image Diffusion", corrected from the draft label). It interprets palettes as **sparse histograms**, exposes an adherence-strength control, and adds a **negative-histogram** mechanism to suppress undesired hues. Relevant to Notebook B if you ever want a learned histogram-conditioned adapter alongside the OT palette loss. In-window, medium impact.

## Perceptual / quality losses

**MILO (arXiv:2509.01411, "MILO: A Lightweight Perceptual Quality Metric for Image and Latent-Space Optimization", Çoğalan/Bemana/Myszkowski/Seidel/Groth, 1 Sep 2025 — verified in-window)** is a lightweight FR-IQA metric trained on **pseudo-MOS** (reproducible distortions scored by an ensemble of modern metrics — no human labels) that doubles as a perceptual *loss* with multi-scale visual-masking. Its notable trick for us: it applies spatial masking to **VAE latents inside Stable Diffusion** with **curriculum** (optimize perceptually-flat regions first, then distorted ones). For our pixel-space Notebook A it slots into `image_anchor_loss` as a masking-aware alternative to DISTS/DreamSim; the latent-masking idea is only relevant if you ever add a latent stage. Maturity: preprint (11 pp, 7 figs); code availability unconfirmed — treat as unproven. Medium impact. Beyond this, the perceptual-metric niche is genuinely quiet: DreamSim/DISTS remain the anchors.

## Multi-task / loss balancing (successors to FAMO/CAGrad/PCGrad)

**MAdam (arXiv:2606.03904, "MAdam: Metric-Aware Multi-Objective Adam", Liu et al. incl. Sabuncu, 2 Jun 2026 — verified in-window)** is the most conceptually interesting: it shows that **every** MOO solver (MGDA/CAGrad/Nash-MTL/FAMO) is silently corrupted when the base optimizer is Adam — a **weighting mismatch** where Adam's second-moment denominator *entangles the time-varying preference vector with gradient statistics*, collapsing distinct trade-offs toward a uniform mixture, plus a **geometric mismatch** where its adaptive metric distorts the Euclidean geometry the solvers assume. MAdam is a **drop-in wrapper** that preconditions the reconciled direction by preference-conditioned curvature so Adam's second moment collapses to identity. This is directly load-bearing for our loss-balancing site: both notebooks balance CLIP + SDS(+shape/palette) with Adam, so any PCGrad/CAGrad we bolt on is subject to exactly this bug. Preprint, code unconfirmed. **GCond (arXiv:2509.07252, "GCond: Gradient Conflict Resolution via Accumulation-based Stabilization for Large-Scale Multi-Task Learning", Limarenko et al., 8 Sep 2025; published IEEE Access vol. 14, 2026 — verified in-window)** is the practical one: PCGrad + gradient-accumulation + an adaptive arbitration mechanism, with a stochastic mode giving ~2× speedup at PCGrad-quality — a cheap FAMO/PCGrad alternative for the vector loop. Also cheap: **AutoScale (arXiv:2508.13979, "AutoScale: Linear Scalarization Guided by Multi-Task Optimization Metrics", Yang et al., 19 Aug 2025)**, metric-guided linear scalarization (near-free vs gradient-surgery methods) — note it is dated **19 Aug 2025, ~2 weeks before the Sep-2025 sweep cutoff** (within the broader 2025H2 window; the draft's "in-window" is marginally off). ConicGrad (2502) is out of window.

## Optimizers & differentiable rendering

The optimizer story is **Muon** maturing in-window — the curvature explanation of *why* it beats Adam (**arXiv:2606.04662, "Why Muon Outperforms Adam: A Curvature Perspective", Wang et al., 3 Jun 2026** — verified in-window: attributes the edge to lower Normalized Directional Sharpness, not update magnitude), plus **schedule-free spectral / SF-NorMuon (arXiv:2605.23061, "Anytime Training with Schedule-Free Spectral Optimization", Apte et al., 21 May 2026** — verified in-window) giving anytime-good checkpoints (useful for LIVE-tier vector rendering where you want a good result at any interrupt). The convergence-bound / critical-batch-size analysis (**arXiv:2507.01598, Sato/Naganuma/Iiduka, 2 Jul 2025**, v5 Jun 2026) is real and correctly described, but its arXiv **predates the Sep-2025 sweep** (Jul 2025 — within 2025H2, so kept as maturation context, not a fresh discovery). Caveat: Muon orthogonalizes *matrix* updates, so it helps MLP-parameterized fields (SGI-style) far more than raw diffvg Bezier/point tensors — honest fit is medium. On differentiable rendering, the concrete image-side advance is **structured 2D-Gaussian representations**: **SGI (arXiv:2603.07789, "SGI: Structured 2D Gaussians for Efficient and Compact Large Image Representation", Pan et al., 8 Mar 2026, CVPR 2026; code released — verified in-window)** imposes seed-based structural regularity with coarse-to-fine fitting (~7.5× compression vs prior non-quantized 2DGS), and **SmartSplat (arXiv:2512.20377, "SmartSplat: Feature-Smart Gaussians for Scalable Compression of Ultra-High-Resolution Images", Li/Zhang/Wang/Shen, 23 Dec 2025; code released — verified in-window)** does feature-smart Gaussians (gradient/color-variance-guided variational sampling) for UHR/8K–16K images — both are strong warm-start / representation upgrades for the Bezier-Splatting side of Notebook B, aesthetic-neutral.
